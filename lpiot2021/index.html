The 1st ACM International Workshop on

No Power and Low Power Internet of Things

With the proliferation of low-power IoT technologies, almost all objects purchased in future will have information to communicate with each other from your juice box to small satellites. While there have been tremendous advances in the capabilities of today’s embedded sensors, from storage, computation to power management, there is a key bottleneck that these sensors face – wireless networking

LP-IoT is a workshop that brings together researchers in the broad field of low power and no energy wireless networking to discuss the next steps towards this future. The workshop will be broad in scope, gathering new ideas and experiences in the realm of low-power and backscatter wireless networks, facilitating discussions on emerging challenges and open questions in the field

All the regular papers will be considered for the Best Paper Award.

In additional to the regular papers, the program of LP-IoT 2021 will also include:

  • A keynote speech by a renowned researcher;
  • A session of industry demos of their products;
  • A panel on a specific topic within the scope of LP-IoT that gathers key experts in this area

    CALL FOR PAPERS

    With the proliferation of low-power IoT technologies, almost all objects purchased in future will have information to communicate with each other from your juice box to small satellites. While there have been tremendous advances in the capabilities of today’s embedded sensors, from storage, computation to power management, there is a key bottleneck that these sensors face – wireless networking

    LP-IoT is a workshop that brings together researchers in the broad field of low power and no energy wireless networking to discuss the next steps towards this future. The workshop will be broad in scope, gathering new ideas and experiences in the realm of low-power and backscatter wireless networks, facilitating discussions on emerging challenges and open questions in the field.

    We are seeking original, previously unpublished papers addressing key issues and challenges in low power wireless networking. All submissions will be judged by their technical merit and relevance to the workshop, based on a thorough review process by the Technical Program Committee.
    Visit the paper submission site to submit your manuscript. Paper length is limited to six (6) pages (in two-column, 10-point format) with figures and everything (There can be 2 additional pages for references). Papers must include the author name and affiliation for single-blind peer reviewing by the program committee. Authors of accepted papers are expected to present their papers at the workshop. Accepted papers will be published in the ACM LP-IoT proceedings and will be archived in the ACM Library. All papers will be considered for the Best Paper Award



    Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Wireless testbeds and prototyping platforms for:
      • Long-Range/Low-Power Wide-Area Wireless Networking
      Backscatter and Energy-Harvesting Networks
      RFID based Networks
      Low-power communication in unconventional scenarios (e.g underwater, space)
  • Security and privacy issues/solutions for above technologies
  • Wireless Sensing and Localization using the above technologies
  • Real World Deployment Experiences from the above technologies
  • Embedded and energy-harvesting systems in the wild
  • Enabling ML and DL applications using above technologies
  • Fairness, equity, and transparency issues
  • Economics of the above technologies
  • Low-Power Spectrum Sensing

    SUBMISSION

    Papers should contain original material and not be previously published or currently submitted for consideration elsewhere. Manuscripts must be submitted by using the HOTCRP conference management system. Prospective authors are encouraged to submit a single PDF file with all font embedded, using the ACM conference proceedings format (use the same PDF formatting guidelines as the main conference). Paper length is limited to six (6) pages (in two-column, 10-point format) with figures and everything (There can be 2 additional pages for references). Papers must include the author name and affiliation for single-blind peer reviewing by the program committee. Authors of accepted papers are expected to present their papers at the workshop. Accepted papers will be published in the ACM LP-IoT proceedings and will be archived in the ACM Library. All papers will be considered for the Best Paper Award.
